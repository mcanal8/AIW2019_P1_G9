The 1923 Great Kanto earthquake struck the Kanto plain on the Japanese main island of Honshu at 11:58:44 am JST on September 1, 1923. Varied accounts hold that the duration of the earthquake was between 4 and 10 minutes. The Kanto quake killed between 100,000 and 140,000 people, making it the deadliest earthquake ever to strike Japan.
